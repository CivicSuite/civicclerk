CivicClerk User Manual
======================

Status: scaffold manual
Version: 0.0.0

Part 1: Non-Technical Overview
------------------------------

CivicClerk will help city clerks manage the legal record of public
meetings: agendas, packets, notices, minutes, votes, motions,
ordinances, resolutions, and public meeting archives.

It is for clerks, deputy clerks, department submitters, city attorneys,
elected officials, board members, residents, and journalists.

The intended workflow is:
1. Create a meeting body.
2. Schedule a meeting.
3. Collect agenda items.
4. Assemble the packet.
5. Track notice deadlines.
6. Capture motions and votes.
7. Draft minutes with citations.
8. Publish approved materials.

CivicClerk is not installable yet. This repository is the starting
scaffold.

Part 2: IT and Technical Overview
---------------------------------

Planned stack:
- Docker-based local deployment
- PostgreSQL 17 + pgvector
- Redis + Celery
- FastAPI backend
- React frontend
- Ollama/Gemma 4 through civiccore.llm
- no telemetry
- no runtime cloud dependency

Part 3: Architecture Reference
------------------------------

CivicClerk owns meeting workflows. It is not electronic voting software,
livestream hosting, a legal decision-maker, or a full records management
system.

Initial data model sketch:
- meeting_bodies
- meetings
- agenda_sections
- agenda_items
- agenda_item_attachments
- packet_versions
- notice_requirements
- notice_postings
- motions
- votes
- minutes_drafts
- minute_citations
- public_comments
- adoption_events
